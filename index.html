Projects

Autonomous Robot Navigation

As part of a team, I programmed a mobile robot 'TurtleBot3' to autonomously naviagate a warehouse environment. I focused on the controls section of the project, implmenting motion algorithms and a PID controller to ensure accurate and stable navigation. A path planning algorithm was developed to allow the robot to navigate around obstacles and reach its destination efficiently. The ROS framework was utilised to communicate dynamically with the robot, as RGB-D sensors were used to detect and avoid obstacles. The projects was successful in demonstrating autononomous navigation, but revealed challenges in traversing real-time environments.

Predictive Power Outage Modelling

Developed a machine learning model to predict severe weather-related power outages in the Greater Sydney region, used to optimse the reliability of residential battery systems. Model was trained on historical weather and outage data that required extensive pre-processing such as geospatial mapping and feature engineering to ensure accurate data representation. A Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) neural network was implemented to detect long-range dependencies in weather patterns. Model acheived high accuracy but limited recall in outage prediction, highlighted key challeng in predicting anomalies such as grid outages in networks.
